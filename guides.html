01 — GETTING STARTED

NEW OPERATOR BRIEFING

Your first 30 minutes in AEGIS. Follow this path to get battle-ready as fast as possible.

01
🎮

LAUNCH & SETTINGS

First, adjust your sensitivity, graphics settings, and audio. Optimal sensitivity: 800 DPI mouse at 5–8 in-game. Set audio to Headphones mode for directional footstep awareness.

💡 Pro tip: Lower your graphics slightly for higher FPS — competitive advantage over visual quality.
→
02
🎯

TRAINING GROUND

Spend 15–20 minutes in Training Ground before your first real match. Practice recoil patterns, movement, and grenade throws against easy bots until movement feels natural.

💡 Use AX-7 Phantom in training — it's the most forgiving AR for learning recoil control.
→
03
🤝

MIXED MODE FIRST

Play Mixed Mode (Players + AI) before jumping into Ranked. This lets you learn map layouts, weapon feel, and game flow against a mix of real and AI opponents at your pace.

💡 Mixed Mode doesn't affect your ranked rating — ideal for practice without pressure.
→
04
🏆

ENTER RANKED

Once comfortable with movement and aiming, enter Ranked Mode. Focus on positioning and survival first. Kills will come naturally as your game sense develops.

💡 Your first 10 ranked matches set your initial MMR — take them seriously.

02 — MOVEMENT SYSTEM

MOVE LIKE AN OPERATOR

AEGIS movement is built for competitive play — fast enough to win, realistic enough to feel earned.

🚶

WALK / RUN

Default movement. Walk is silent — use it to avoid revealing your position. Run is fast but audible to nearby enemies. Switch constantly based on game state.

Silent walking is a major competitive edge in close-quarters maps.
💨

SPRINT

Maximum speed. In Ranked Mode, sprinting drains stamina — use strategically. Sprinting lowers your weapon and reduces accuracy. Never sprint into combat directly.

Stamina drain in Ranked Mode — manage your sprint distance.
🦆

CROUCH

Reduces your hitbox and dramatically improves accuracy. Footsteps become silent while crouching. Essential for holding angles and winning close-range gunfights.

Crouch + ADS = maximum accuracy. Use for holding power positions.
🪖

PRONE

Lowest profile, best accuracy bonus. Extremely difficult to hit. Essential for long-range engagements and defensive play. Very slow mobility — use only when stationary.

Prone makes you nearly invisible but completely commits your position.
🧗

VAULT / MANTLE

Automatically vaults over small obstacles and mantles up to ledges. Critical for map traversal and reaching power positions. Practice vault paths on each map.

Learn vault shortcuts on Frostline bunkers and Ember Rain rooftops.
🏃

JUMP

Standard jump. Bunny-hopping is penalized — jumping reduces accuracy significantly. Use jumps for obstacles only, not as an evasion tactic in gunfights.

Never jump during a gunfight — accuracy drops to near zero mid-air.
MOVEMENT QUICK TIPS
Strafe left-right while shooting to make yourself harder to hit
Crouch at the end of a sprint to peek corners without stopping momentum
Sound is a weapon — crouch-walk when pushing a flanking route
Lean around corners to check angles without exposing your full body
03 — SHOOTING SYSTEM

MASTER THE GUNPLAY

Understanding the shooting system is the difference between a casual player and an elite operator.

RECOIL SYSTEM

DYNAMIC RECOIL PATTERNS

Every weapon has a unique recoil pattern — a combination of vertical climb and horizontal drift. Master the pattern by holding opposite mouse movement while firing.

SPRAY PATTERN EXAMPLE — AX-7 PHANTOM
+
Pull mouse DOWN + opposite direction of drift to control spray
First 3 bullets are accurate — tap-fire at long range
ADS reduces recoil by ~30% across all weapons
Full auto beyond 40m = wasted ammo on most ARs

KNOW YOUR ARSENAL

Each weapon class serves a purpose. Choose the right tool for the right engagement.

ASSAULT RIFLE
🔫

The backbone of AEGIS combat. Effective at all ranges when used correctly. Best for players who want consistent, reliable damage output.

STRENGTHS

Recoil at range

SHOTGUNS

Highest close-range burst damage. Devastating within 10 meters, completely ineffective beyond 25. Combine with movement — rush, shoot, retreat. One-shot body at point blank.

05 — RANKED MODE TIPS

CLIMB THE RANKS

Ranked Mode in AEGIS rewards smart play over aggression. These principles separate good players from great ones.

01

POSITIONING OVER KILLS

Players who survive longer accumulate more points than those who rush for kills and die early. Always ask: "Is this position safe?" before engaging.

02

USE THE MINIMAP

Check your minimap every 3–5 seconds. Gunshots and enemy positions appear briefly. Never be surprised by a flank you could have anticipated.

03

SOUND IS INTELLIGENCE

Footsteps, reloads, grenade pins — every sound tells you something. Use headphones. Silence your own movement when gathering intel on enemy positions.

04

MANAGE STAMINA IN RANKED

Ranked Mode has a stamina system. Don't waste it sprinting to bad positions. Conserve stamina for when you actually need to reposition fast under fire.

05

PLAY YOUR FIRST 10 CAREFULLY

Your first 10 ranked matches set your initial MMR bracket for the season. Treat them like a tournament — these matches have the highest individual weight.

06

ONE WEAPON MASTERY

Master one primary weapon before branching out. Knowing one recoil pattern deeply outperforms being average with several. Pick your weapon, own it.

07

EQUIP PROPERLY

Always carry one lethal (frag grenade ideal) and one tactical (smoke for cover or flash for pushes). Grenades win more gunfights than many players realize.

08

CROUCH AT THE RIGHT TIME

Crouch when holding an angle, never when moving to a position. A crouching player is easy to track once the crouch timing is known — mix it up.

06 — MAP STRATEGIES

KNOW YOUR TERRAIN

Each operation has its own identity, flow, and dominant positions. Learn the terrain before the enemy does.

OP-FL-01
FROSTLINE
SNOW BATTLEFIELD
LONG-RANGE SNIPERS OPEN TERRAIN

KEY POSITION: The frozen lake center. Control this and you control the map's key sightlines. Use snow trenches to push safely.

WEAPON CHOICE: Snipers and DMRs dominate open areas. Bring an AR with medium scope as backup for bunker engagements.

COUNTER: Use smoke grenades on the lake crossing to push through sniper sightlines safely.

OP-BD-02
BLACK DUNE
DESERT OIL REFINERY
MULTI-LAYER VERTICAL COMBAT SANDSTORM EVENTS

KEY POSITION: The oil rig upper platform. Vertical advantage wins here. Control the high ground and all lower routes funnel into your crossfire.

SANDSTORM TIP: When sandstorms hit, close engagement distances. Switch to SMG or shotgun and use sound for tracking.

UNDERGROUND: Tunnels under the refinery offer safe rotation. Learn the tunnel network to appear where enemies don't expect.

OP-IW-03
IRON WOLF
MOUNTAIN PASS
SNIPER MAP NARROW PATHS FOG CONDITION

KEY POSITION: The radio tower summit. Highest point on the map. Whoever holds it can see and reach anywhere — contest it aggressively.

FOG MECHANIC: Fog reduces visibility to ~40m in low areas. Mountain paths stay clear. Use caves for safe low-visibility rotation.

COUNTER-SNIPE: Bring smoke grenades to push sniper positions. One smoke on the radio tower forces them to move.

OP-ER-04
EMBER RAIN
BURNING CITY
CQB DOMINANT VERTICALITY DYNAMIC SMOKE

KEY POSITION: Collapsed skyscraper rooftop. Risky to reach but rewards with a full-city overview and multiple angle options.

SMOKE MECHANIC: Dynamic smoke reduces visibility in specific zones. Don't fight through smoke — rotate around it.

SUBWAY TUNNELS: The fastest rotation route on the map. Learn the 3 tunnel exits to appear behind enemy lines.

UNIVERSAL MAP PRINCIPLES
01 Always know at least 2 rotation routes from any position before committing to it
02 Power positions come with risk — if you can see everyone, everyone can see you
03 Watch the minimap for enemy sound markers — predict flanks before they happen
04 Environmental audio tells a story — learn what normal sounds like so abnormal stands out
